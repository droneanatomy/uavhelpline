The story of drone delivery has always been less about the aircraft and more about the airspace around it. The latest expansion of trials into denser corridors reflects steady progress on the harder problem: operating safely where people and buildings are.

## Technical Breakdown

- **Operation:** corridor-based routing that constrains where aircraft fly and simplifies the airspace picture.
- **Detect-and-avoid:** the enabling capability, increasingly a mix of onboard sensing and networked traffic awareness.
- **Range / endurance:** short, repeatable hops favour lighter platforms with quick turnaround over long-range designs.

## Industry Impact

For **operators**, denser corridors mean more addressable demand per launch site. For **regulators**, corridor models offer a tractable way to grant access without opening all airspace at once. For **integrators**, the opportunity is the logistics software that schedules and deconflicts flights. The trajectory points toward delivery as a routine utility rather than a demonstration.
