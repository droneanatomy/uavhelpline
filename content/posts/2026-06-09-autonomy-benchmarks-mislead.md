Autonomy is the most marketed and least standardised capability in the drone market. A platform that demos flawlessly in an open field can struggle in a cluttered industrial site — and the spec sheet rarely captures the difference.

## Technical Breakdown

- **Autonomy level:** vendor labels collapse very different capabilities into a single number; the conditions matter more than the level.
- **Failure modes:** low light, feature-poor scenes, and dense obstacles separate robust systems from fragile ones.
- **Test design:** the only reliable benchmark is the buyer's own environment, flown repeatedly, measured for intervention rate.

## Industry Impact

For **operators**, the lesson is to pilot before they purchase, against their real sites. For **manufacturers**, durable advantage comes from honest reliability data, not headline autonomy levels. For **investors**, the companies publishing transparent intervention metrics are the ones building defensible trust. The market matures when reliability, not marketing, becomes the unit of comparison.
