For years, the heavy lifting of drone perception happened on the ground, with the aircraft acting as a flying sensor. More capable edge processors are changing that balance, pushing inference onto the platform itself.

## Technical Breakdown

- **Compute:** efficient onboard accelerators run detection and tracking within the aircraft's power budget.
- **Latency:** local inference removes the round-trip to a ground station, sharpening real-time response.
- **Resilience:** onboard perception keeps working when the link degrades — a meaningful reliability gain.

## Industry Impact

For **manufacturers**, onboard AI becomes a hardware-software differentiator that is hard to copy. For **operators**, lower latency and link-independent autonomy widen the set of viable missions. For **regulators**, processing data onboard rather than streaming it has favourable privacy implications. The centre of gravity for drone intelligence is moving from the ground to the airframe.
